## 🧠 How the "Magic" Works (The Data Routine)

You never have to manually download data. PatternOS runs a "Data Heartbeat" every single day.

### **The Daily Cycle**
1.  **The Morning Fetch (6:00 AM IST)**: The system automatically wakes up and reaches out to official sources (AMFI, Yahoo Finance, NSE) to grab the latest prices and NAVs.
2.  **The Cleaning Phase**: It removes errors, fills in gaps, and organizes everything into a clean, searchable database.
3.  **The Intelligence Layer**: The system runs its "math engines" to calculate trends, identify patterns, and update your dashboard.

### **The 4 Levels of Intelligence**
Every piece of information in PatternOS has a "Level." As you go deeper, the intelligence gets smarter:
* **Level 1 (Raw)**: Just the numbers (e.g., "The price is ₹1500").
* **Level 2 (Technical)**: The numbers + trends (e.g., "The price is ₹1500 and it's trending up").
* **Level 3 (Contextual)**: Trends + surroundings (e.g., "The price is trending up, and the whole IT sector is also rising").
* **Level 4 (Cognitive/AI)**: The "Why" (e.g., "The price is rising because of a specific pattern, supported by sector momentum and high institutional interest").

## 📖 Simple Glossary (For Non-Traders)

* **NAV (Net Asset Value)**: The "price" of one unit of a mutual fund.
* **RSI (Relative Strength Index)**: A tool that tells you if a stock is "overbought" (too expensive) or "oversold" (a potential bargain).
* **MACD**: A tool that helps identify when a stock's momentum is changing direction.
* **Open Interest (OI)**: The total number of active contracts in the market; it shows how much "interest" there is in a particular price level.
* **PCR (Put-Call Ratio)**: A sentiment gauge. High PCR usually means investors are hedging against a drop (Bearish), while low PCR means they are betting on a rise (Bullish).
* **Expense Ratio**: The annual fee you pay to the mutual fund company to manage your money.
